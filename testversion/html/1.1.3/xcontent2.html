Chapter 1 Elementary Arithmetic - Section 1.3 Transformation of terms

1.3.3 Exercieces

Exercise 1.3.9  
Simplify the following terms for appropriate numbers a, b, x, y, z:
  1. 3x-6x y2 +4xyz -2x  =  .


    3x-6x y2 +4xyz -2x   =  - 3 2 +3 y2 -2yz .



  2. (3a-2b)·(4a-6)  =  .


    (3a-2b)·(4a-6)  =  12 a2 -18a-8ab+12b .



  3. (2a+3b )2 -(3a-2b )2  =  .


    (2a+3b )2 -(3a-2b )2 = (4 a2 +12ab+9 b2 )-(9 a2 -12ab+4 b2 )  = -5 a2 +24ab+5 b2  .



  4. 3a 3a+6b + 2b a+2b  =  .
    Adding up the numerators over the least common denominator gives 3a 3a+6b + 2b a+2b = 3a 3a+6b + 6b 3a+6b = 3a+6b 3a+6b =1.


The input must not contain any brackets.


Exercise 1.3.10  
For the following exercises a little more patience is required. Simplify:
  1. 1 2 x(4x+3y)+ 3 2 (5 x2 -6xy)  =  .


    1 2 x(4x+3y)+ 3 2 (5 x2 -6xy)  =  2 x2 + 3 2 xy+ 15 2 x2 -9xy  =   19 2 x2 - 15 2 xy .



  2. 18 x2 -48xy+32 y2 12y-9x · 18x+24y 9 x2 -16 y2  =  .
    Simplifying the expression gives

    18 x2 -48xy+32 y2 12y-9x · 18x+24y 9 x2 -16 y2 = 4· 9 x2 -24xy+16 y2 4y-3x · 3x+4y 9 x2 -16 y2 = 4· (3x-4y )2 4y-3x · 3x+4y 9 x2 -16 y2 = -4· (3x-4y )2 3x-4y · 3x+4y (3x+4y)(3x-4y) =-4 .



  3. ( a2 +5a-2)(2 a2 -3a-9)-( 1 2 a2 +3a-5)( a2 -4a+3)  =  .


    ( a2 +5a-2)(2 a2 -3a-9)-( 1 2 a2 +3a-5)( a2 -4a+3)            



    = 2 a4 +10 a3 -4 a2 -3 a3 -15 a2 +6a-9 a2 -45a+18       -( 1 2 a4 +3 a3 -5 a2 -2 a3 -12 a2 +20a+ 3 2 a2 +9a-15)    = 3 2 a4 +6 a3 - 25 2 a2 -68a+33 .



The input must not contain any brackets.


Exercise 1.3.11  
Use a binomial formula to calculate the following squares:
  1. 432  =  .


    432   =  (40+3 )2   =   402 +2·40·3+ 32   =  1600+240+9  =  1849 .



  2. 972  =  .


    972   =  (90+7 )2   =   902 +2·90·7+ 72   =  8100+1260+49  =  9409 .



  3. 412 - 382  =  .


    412 - 382   =  (41+38)(41-38)  =  79·3  =  237 .





Exercise 1.3.12  
Apply a binomial formula to expand the product and collect like terms:
  1. (-5xy-2 )2  =  .


    (-5xy-2 )2   =  (-1 )2 ·(5xy+2 )2   =  25 x2 y2 +20xy+4 .



  2. (-6ab+7bc)(-6ab-7bc)  =  .


    (-6ab+7bc)(-6ab-7bc)  =  (-6ab )2 -(7bc )2   =  36 a2 b2 -49 b2 c2  .



  3. (-6ab+7bc)(-6ab+7bc)  =  .


    (-6ab+7bc)(-6ab+7bc)  =  (-6ab+7bc )2   =  36 a2 b2 -84a b2 c+49 b2 c2  .



  4. ( x2 +3)(- x2 -3)  =  .


    ( x2 +3)(- x2 -3)  =  -( x2 +3 )2   =  - x4 -6 x2 -9 .



The input must not contain any brackets.
Exercise 1.3.13  
Factorise the following terms as far as possible using one of the binomial formulas:
  1. 4 x2 +12xy+9 y2  =  .


    4 x2 +12xy+9 y2   =  (2x+3y )2  .



  2. 64 a2 -96a+36  =  .


    64 a2 -96a+36  =  (8a-6 )2  .



  3. 25 x2 -16 y2 +15x+12y  =  .


    25 x2 -16 y2 +15x+12y = (5x )2 -(4y )2 +3·(5x+4y)  = (5x+4y)(5x-4y)+3·(5x+4y)  = (5x+4y)(5x-4y+3) .



Factorise the result until it fits into the input field.
