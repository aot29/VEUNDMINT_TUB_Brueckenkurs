1.2.3 Exercieces



Exercise 1.2.218  
Reduce the following fractions to the lowest terms:

  1. 216 240  =  .
    Since ggT(216,240)=24 we have 216 240 = 216:24 240:24 = 9 10 .


  2. 36 72  =  .
    36 divides 72, hence 36 72 = 1 2 .


  3. 48 144  =  .
    48 divides 144, hence 48 144 = 1 3 .


  4. -a+2b -4b+2a  =  if a not equals .
    Reducing gives -a+2b -4b+2a = (-1)·(-2b+a) 2·(-2b+a) =- 1 2 . The fraction is only defined for a≠2b.


Enter the fraction as numerator/denominator fully reduced and with positive denominator. Always place the signs in front of the fractions, and do not use brackets.


Exercise 1.2.219  
Calculate and fully reduce the following expressions for appropriate numbers a,b,x,y:
  1. 1 2 - 2 7 + 3 8 + 3 4  =  .
    Adding up the numerators over the least common denominator gives 1 2 - 2 7 + 3 8 + 3 4 = 28 56 - 16 56 + 21 56 + 42 56 = 75 56 , since ggT(2,7,8,4)=56.


  2. 3 13 : 7 26  =  .
    Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal: 3 13 : 7 26 = 3 13 · 26 7 = 3·26 13·7 = 3·2 1·7 = 6 7 .


  3. (1. 4 ‾ ·3- 1 2 )· 6 7  =  .
    Converting the decimal expression to a fraction gives (1. 4 ‾ ·3- 1 2 )· 6 7 =(1. 4 ‾ ·18-3)· 1 7 =(26-3)· 1 7 = 23 7 .


Enter the fractions fully reduced and with positive numerator.


Exercise 1.2.220  
Convert the following infinite repeating decimal fractions to fractions and fully reduce them:
  1. 0. 4 ‾  =  .

  2. 0. 23 ‾  =  .

  3. 0.12 34 ‾  =  .

  4. 0. 9 ‾  =  .

Enter the fractions fully reduced and with positive numerator.
 
Using the trick for converting infinite repeating decimal numbers one gets the following solutions:
  • x=0. 4 ‾ , hence 10x-x=4  ⇒  9x=4  ⇒  x= 4 9 ,

  • x=0. 23 ‾ , hence 100x-x=23  ⇒  99x=23  ⇒  x= 23 99 ,

  • x=0.12 34 ‾ , hence 100x-x=12.22  ⇒  99x= 1222 100   ⇒  x= 1222 9900 = 611 4950 ,

  • x=0. 9 ‾ , hence 10x-x=9  ⇒  9x=9  ⇒  x=1.

Note for the last part of this exercise, that 1=1.000… and 1=0.999…=0. 9 ‾ are two decimal representations of one and the same number.
